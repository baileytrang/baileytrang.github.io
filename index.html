How much can we trust the medical systems?
How reliable are AI predictions?
How might AI assist in equalizing worldwide medical services?

Inspired by these questions, my research interest lies in causal discovery in healthcare and human diseases. My goal is to facilitate trustworthy AI that enhances quality balance and equity in global healthcare services.

I'm currently working closely with Prof. Naoaki Okazaki for my Master's, where we introduce a counterfactual learning algorithm to enhance Visual Question Answering on real-life and medical images.

Additionally, I've been engaged in a long-term internship at Mila, supervised by Prof. Yoshua Bengio and Prof. Dianbo Liu. Our primary focus is on System2 and cognition-inspired AI, with an emphasis on causality and generalization.

Soon, I'll be joining the National University of Singapore as a Research Assistant at Prof. Dianbo Liu's CogAI4Sci Lab, while maintaining collaboration with Mila team. Our work will center on leveraging causality for healthcare.

I'm actively looking for PhD opportunities at the intersection of AI, causal discovery, and healthcare equity.
